How to Build a PC: Step‑by‑Step Guide

Follow this detailed, beginner‑friendly guide to assemble your own computer safely and correctly.

0

Before You Start

📦 What You'll Need

  • Components: CPU, motherboard, RAM, GPU, storage, PSU, case, cooler
  • Tools: Phillips #2 screwdriver (magnetic tip helps)
  • Optional but recommended: Anti‑static wrist strap, cable ties, thermal paste (if not pre‑applied)
  • Workspace: Clean, flat surface with good lighting
💡

Pro Tip: Touch a metal part of your case before handling components to discharge static. Better yet, use an anti‑static wrist strap.

1

Install the CPU

The CPU is the brain of your computer. Handle it carefully – avoid touching the gold pins or contacts.

  1. Open the CPU socket lever on the motherboard.
  2. Align the small triangle on the CPU corner with the triangle on the socket.
  3. Gently lower the CPU into place – it should drop in with zero force.
  4. Close the lever to secure the CPU.
⚠️

Never force the CPU. If it doesn't sit perfectly, check alignment again.

2

Install RAM (Memory)

RAM provides temporary storage for running programs. Install it in the correct slots for best performance.

  1. Open the clips on the RAM slots.
  2. Align the notch in the RAM stick with the slot's key.
  3. Press firmly and evenly until the clips snap into place. You should hear a click.
  4. For dual‑channel, use slots 2 and 4 (or 1 and 3) – check your motherboard manual.
💡

Pro Tip: If you have only one stick, use the slot labeled “DIMM_A2” or second from the CPU.

3

Install the M.2 SSD (Storage)

Modern SSDs are tiny and install directly on the motherboard.

  1. Locate the M.2 slot (usually under a heatsink on newer boards).
  2. Remove the screw from the standoff.
  3. Insert the SSD at a 30‑degree angle, then press it flat and secure with the screw.
  4. If you're using a 2.5” SATA drive, mount it in the drive cage later.
4

Install the CPU Cooler

Keep your CPU cool for stable performance and longevity.

  1. If your cooler requires a backplate, install it behind the motherboard.
  2. Apply a pea‑sized dot of thermal paste if not pre‑applied.
  3. Mount the cooler according to its manual – usually with screws or a clip mechanism.
  4. Connect the fan cable to the CPU_FAN header on the motherboard.
💡

Pro Tip: Don't overdo thermal paste – a small dot in the center spreads perfectly when the cooler is tightened.

5

Prepare the Case & Install Motherboard

Now it's time to move everything into the case.

  1. Install the I/O shield (the metal plate with cutouts) into the case from the inside.
  2. Screw in standoffs that match your motherboard's mounting holes.
  3. Carefully lower the motherboard into place, aligning with the I/O shield.
  4. Secure with the provided screws. Don't overtighten.
  5. Connect the front panel cables (power switch, reset, LEDs) – consult your motherboard manual for the exact pins.
6

Install the Power Supply (PSU)

The PSU delivers power to every component.

  1. Mount the PSU in its designated spot – usually bottom or top of the case, fan facing outward.
  2. Route necessary cables through the back of the case for a clean look.
  3. Connect the main 24‑pin motherboard cable, the CPU power (4+4 pin), and later the GPU cables.
⚠️

Use only the cables that came with your PSU. Mixing modular cables can damage components.

7

Install the Graphics Card (GPU)

The GPU handles all visuals – essential for gaming and creative work.

  1. Remove the PCIe slot covers on the back of the case where the GPU ports will stick out.
  2. Open the PCIe latch on the motherboard.
  3. Insert the GPU into the top PCIe x16 slot until you hear a click.
  4. Secure it with screws to the case.
  5. Connect the required power cables from the PSU (6+2 pin connectors).
8

Connect Storage Drives (2.5” / 3.5”)

If you have additional SATA SSDs or hard drives, install them now.

  1. Mount drives in the drive cage or designated slots using screws or tool‑less brackets.
  2. Connect a SATA data cable from the drive to the motherboard.
  3. Connect a SATA power cable from the PSU to the drive.
9

Cable Management & Final Checks

Tidy cables improve airflow and make the build look professional.

  1. Use zip ties or velcro straps to bundle cables neatly behind the motherboard tray.
  2. Ensure no cables are touching fan blades or blocking airflow.
  3. Double‑check all connections: 24‑pin, CPU power, GPU power, front panel headers, fan cables.
  4. Close both side panels.
10

First Boot & BIOS Setup

The moment of truth – power on your new machine.

  1. Connect your monitor, keyboard, and mouse.
  2. Flip the PSU switch to “on”.
  3. Press the case power button.
  4. If it boots, enter BIOS (usually by pressing Del or F2) to check temperatures and boot order.
  5. Install your operating system via USB drive.
💡

Pro Tip: If nothing happens, check the front panel connectors and ensure the PSU is switched on. Don't panic – most issues are simple cable mistakes.

Congratulations! You've built your own PC. Enjoy the satisfaction and performance of a custom machine.
